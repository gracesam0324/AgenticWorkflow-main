## Business Direction — Aggressive Growth (local-first, non-SaaS)

### Monetization models + pricing anchors (offline-friendly)
- **Per-user perpetual license**
  - Anchor: \$149–\$299 (personal), \$499–\$999 (pro)
- **Annual “Updates + Support”** (optional)
  - Anchor: 25–40% of license price/year
- **Per-seat subscription (billing only; still offline)**
  - Anchor: \$29–\$79/seat/month (paid annually) with offline license files
- **Enterprise offline pack (air-gapped)**
  - Anchor: \$25k–\$200k/year + \$10k–\$50k onboarding
- **Paid Packs** (templates/connectors)
  - Template packs: \$49–\$199; Connector packs: \$99–\$499
- **Services**
  - Anchor: \$2k–\$15k per engagement

Recommended aggressive mix: **perpetual license + optional updates plan + packs**, add enterprise offline once 2–3 org signals appear.

---

### Go-to-market to first 100 users
- **Wedges**
  - Senior devs/tech leads (implementation + refactor)
  - Security/infra teams needing offline automation
  - Indie builders avoiding SaaS lock-in
- **Positioning**
  - “Offline agentic workflow automation that actually implements changes on your machine—repeatable, auditable, and fast.”
- **Channels**
  - Founder-led outbound (targeted)
  - Short technical demos (issue → plan → local diff)
  - Community distribution (HN/Reddit/local-first communities)
  - Consultants/MSPs (revenue share on licenses)
- **Conversion**
  - Trial: 14-day full-featured or “10 successful runs” meter (local, no signup)
  - Time-to-value: first win in <30 minutes via guided starter workflows

---

### 6-month KPI targets (measurable)
- Installs: 100–300
- Activated users (≥1 successful run): 100
- Activation rate: 40%
- Median time-to-first-success: 30 minutes
- 4-week retention: 30%
- Median successful runs/week among retained: 5
- Top-3 workflows “manual fix required”: <10%
- Revenue by month 6: \$25k–\$75k; ARPA \$250–\$500
- Teams (≥3 seats): 5–15 or enterprise pilots: 1–3

---

### Feature priorities to win fast
1) **Safe code changes**: diff/rollback/dry-run + audit trail + deterministic verification
2) **Onboarding-first win**: guided run + starter workflows
3) **Workflow packs** (offline installable) + versioned templates
4) **Local policy/guardrails**: allowlists, approvals, secret detection
5) **Team/offline enterprise needs** without SaaS: shared templates via file/Git; audit exports

---

### Biggest risks (aggressive)
- Trust failure (repo break / secret leak)
- Scope creep
- Support burden from environment diversity
- Pricing/packaging confusion
- Enterprise drag (procurement/security cycles)
- “agentic hype” perception if outcomes aren’t repeatable
